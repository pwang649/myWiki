Convex Functions​

We begin with a definition of the graph of a real-valued function.

Definition​

The graph of f:Ω→R,Ω⊂Rnf: \Omega \rightarrow \mathbb{R}, \Omega \subset \mathbb{R}^nf:Ω→R,Ω⊂Rn, is the set of points in Ω×R⊂Rn+1\Omega \times \mathbb{R} \subset \mathbb{R}^{n+1}Ω×R⊂Rn+1 given by

{[xf(x)]:x∈Ω}.\left\{\left[\begin{array}{c} \boldsymbol{x} \\ f(\boldsymbol{x}) \end{array}\right]: \boldsymbol{x} \in \Omega\right\} .{[xf(x)​]:x∈Ω}.

We can visualize the graph of fff as simply the set of points on a "plot" of f(x)f(\boldsymbol{x})f(x) versus x\boldsymbol{x}x.

epi⁡(f)={[xβ]:x∈Ω,β∈R,β≥f(x)}.\operatorname{epi}(f)=\left\{\left[\begin{array}{l} \boldsymbol{x} \\ \beta \end{array}\right]: \boldsymbol{x} \in \Omega, \beta \in \mathbb{R}, \beta \geq f(\boldsymbol{x})\right\} .epi(f)={[xβ​]:x∈Ω,β∈R,β≥f(x)}.

The epigraph epi (f)(f)(f) of a function fff is simply the set of points in Ω×R\Omega \times \mathbb{R}Ω×R on or above the graph of fff. We can also think of epi (f)(f)(f) as a subset of Rn+1\mathbb{R}^{n+1}Rn+1.

We now introduce the notion of a convex function.

Definition​

A function f:Ω→R,Ω⊂Rnf: \Omega \rightarrow \mathbb{R}, \Omega \subset \mathbb{R}^nf:Ω→R,Ω⊂Rn, is convex on Ω\OmegaΩ if its epigraph is a convex set.

If a function f:Ω→R,Ω⊂Rnf: \Omega \rightarrow \mathbb{R}, \Omega \subset \mathbb{R}^nf:Ω→R,Ω⊂Rn, is convex on Ω\OmegaΩ, then Ω\OmegaΩ is a convex set.

The next theorem gives a very useful characterization of convex functions. This characterization is often used as a definition for a convex function.

Theorem​

A function f:Ω→Rf: \Omega \rightarrow \mathbb{R}f:Ω→R defined on a convex set Ω⊂Rn\Omega \subset \mathbb{R}^nΩ⊂Rn is convex if and only if for all x,y∈Ω\boldsymbol{x}, \boldsymbol{y} \in \Omegax,y∈Ω and all α∈(0,1)\alpha \in(0,1)α∈(0,1), we have

f(αx+(1−α)y)≤αf(x)+(1−α)f(y)f(\alpha \boldsymbol{x}+(1-\alpha) \boldsymbol{y}) \leq \alpha f(\boldsymbol{x})+(1-\alpha) f(\boldsymbol{y})f(αx+(1−α)y)≤αf(x)+(1−α)f(y)

Proof​

⇐\Leftarrow⇐ : Assume that for all x,y∈Ω\boldsymbol{x}, \boldsymbol{y} \in \Omegax,y∈Ω and α∈(0,1)\alpha \in(0,1)α∈(0,1)

f(αx+(1−α)y)≤αf(x)+(1−α)f(y).f(\alpha \boldsymbol{x}+(1-\alpha) \boldsymbol{y}) \leq \alpha f(\boldsymbol{x})+(1-\alpha) f(\boldsymbol{y}) .f(αx+(1−α)y)≤αf(x)+(1−α)f(y).

Let [x⊤,a]⊤\left[\boldsymbol{x}^{\top}, a\right]^{\top}[x⊤,a]⊤ and [y⊤,b]⊤\left[\boldsymbol{y}^{\top}, b\right]^{\top}[y⊤,b]⊤ be two points in epi (f)(f)(f), where a,b∈Ra, b \in \mathbb{R}a,b∈R. From the definition of epi (f)(f)(f) it follows that

Convex optimization problems in standard form​

A convex optimization problem is one of the form

 minimize f0(x) subject to fi(x)≤0,i=1,…,maiTx=bi,i=1,…,p,\begin{array}{ll} \text { minimize } & f_0(x) \\ \text { subject to } & f_i(x) \leq 0, \quad i=1, \ldots, m \\ & a_i^T x=b_i, \quad i=1, \ldots, p, \end{array} minimize  subject to ​f0​(x)fi​(x)≤0,i=1,…,maiT​x=bi​,i=1,…,p,​

where f0,…,fmf_0, \ldots, f_mf0​,…,fm​ are convex functions. The convex problem has three additional requirements:

  • the objective function must be convex,
